# Downloaded Papers

This directory contains research papers for the project "An LLM That's Careful With Its Words".

1. **Let's Think Dot by Dot: Hidden Computation in Transformer Language Models**
   - **Authors**: Jacob Pfau, William Merrill, Samuel R. Bowman
   - **Year**: 2024
   - **arXiv**: [2404.15758](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.15758)
   - **Why relevant**: Shows that meaningless filler tokens can enable hidden computation in transformers, potentially leading to more "careful" reasoning.

2. **Think before you speak: Training Language Models With Pause Tokens**
   - **Authors**: Sachin Goyal et al.
   - **Year**: 2023
   - **arXiv**: [2310.02226](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.02226)
   - **Why relevant**: Introduces the [PAUSE] token, showing that allowing models extra computation steps before generating results in better reasoning.

3. **Quiet-STaR: Language Models Can Teach Themselves to Think Before Speaking**
   - **Authors**: Eric Zelikman et al.
   - **Year**: 2024
   - **arXiv**: [2403.09629](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.09629)
   - **Why relevant**: Extends the STaR framework to generate internal thoughts for general text, not just QA, improving overall generation quality.

4. **Pause Tokens Strictly Increase the Expressivity of Constant-Depth Transformers**
   - **Authors**: Charles London, Varun Kanade
   - **Year**: 2025
   - **arXiv**: [2505.21024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.21024)
   - **Why relevant**: Provides theoretical proof that adding pause tokens increases the computational power of transformers.

5. **Rethinking Thinking Tokens: Understanding Why They Underperform in Practice**
   - **Authors**: Sreeram Vennam et al.
   - **Year**: 2024
   - **arXiv**: [2411.11371](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.11371)
   - **Why relevant**: Discusses the limitations of the thinking token approach, such as noisy gradients from single-embedding tokens, offering key design considerations for the experiment.
